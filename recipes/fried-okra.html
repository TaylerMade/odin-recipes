Fried Okra Recipe

A Southern Treat

This traditional Southern Fried Okra recipe features fresh (or frozen and thawed) okra. They're sliced and dredged in egg and flour, then deep fried to crunchy golden perfection. Maybe not the healthiest side, but these pop-able okra pieces are tasty and irresistible... And isn't that what really matters?

Ingredients:

  • Fresh or frozen okra (whole pods)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Spices: salt, garlic powder, paprika, black pepper, and/or cayenne
  • Egg and milk
  • Fry oil (peanut, canola, or soy)

Steps:

  1. Set a large, heavy pot over medium-high heat. Pour the oil into the pot and place a cooking thermometer on the side of the pot, with the tip down in the oil
  2. When the oil temperature reaches 375F degrees, turn the heat down to medium to maintain
  3. In a shallow bowl, mix the cornmeal, flour, 1 teaspoon salt, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and pepper
  4. In a second bowl, whisk together the egg, milk, and 1/2 teaspoon salt
  5. Cut the tops off the okra pods. Then cut each pod into half-inch rounds
  6. Add the chopped okra pieces to the egg mixture and toss the okra pieces in the mixture to coat
  7. Drain off the egg mixture and add the pieces to the bowl with the breading mixture and, again, toss to coat
  8. Shake the excess breading off the okra and gently place half in the fry oil. Stir and fry for 3-5 minutes or until the okra is golden brown
  9. Use a skimmer to move the breaded fried okra to the holding plate. Then repeat with the second batch
  10. Serve immediately or keep warm using an oven on low temperature
